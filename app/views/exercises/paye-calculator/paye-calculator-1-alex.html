PAYE Tax Calculator

What to expect

Use this service to estimate how much Income Tax and National Insurance you should pay for the current tax year (6 April 2022 to 5 April 2023).

You may be able to claim a refund if you’ve paid too much tax.

{{ govukInsetText({ text: "For the purposes of this prototype, questions are asked with the intention the calculator is only available to residents in England." }) }} {{ govukButton({ text: "Start now", href: "/exercises/paye-calculator/paye-calculator-2-alex.html", isStartButton: true }) }}

Before you start

You cannot get an estimate online if:

  • you’re not a pay as you earn (PAYE) taxpayer
  • your only income is from self employment or state benefits, such as State Pension
  • you contribute to a pension scheme through your employer
  • you’re repaying a student loan
{{ govukInsetText({ text: "If you have more than one job, use the calculator once for each job." }) }}

There are different ways to:

  • check your Income Tax payments for the current year
  • check how much you paid last year (6 April 2021 to 5 April 2022)
  • estimate how much you should have paid in a previous year

You can check you’ve paid the right tax by contacting HMRC or by getting help from an accountant.
